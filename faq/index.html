I’d like to start with one UNIbrace product, which one should I choose first?

This is a difficult question as there are many variables and tradeoffs. If you are looking for the maximum rigidity from one product then the UNIbrace XB/XBQ is your best choice. XB Series Questions

Do I need to purchase the M8 HD nutsert installation tool?

You do not need to purchase the tool from us, but you absolutely need a proper M8 nutsert installation tool. Please DO NOT make a tool from a standard bolt and nuts. The vast majority of people who have had installation issues have been due to not using a proper tool. These tools, and other nutsert installation tools are made from hardened steel or have a means of getting a large amount of torque needed to set the nutsert properly. Homemade tools do not. We understand not wanting to spend extra money, but you will regret this choice!

Which installation guide is the best to use?

We have several XB Series installation guides. They fall into two categories: 1. Standard, which require interior panel removal and 2. Those that do not require the removal of the interior panels. Many customers ask which is the best to use? It really depends on your skill and how much time you have. We still recommend using the standard type installation where the interior panels are removed because they they give you more flexibility in brace placement to better avoid spot weld locations when drilling for the nutserts. With the panels removed you can better see all the spot welds and reposition the brace up or down as necessary. However, this method takes more time and requires more skill in removal and replacement of the interior panels. Either way, please take your time, and check your measurements before drilling. Always use a center punch to locate your holes and the best method is to start with one hole, get the nutsert installed and use the brace as a template to locate the next hole. Repeat this method for the third and fourth hole. Most importantly, follow the instructions.

What is the additional cost for the Jetta/Bora specific UNIbrace XB models, what is the lead-time and why do they cost more?

The Jetta/Bora specific UNIbrace XB’s, model J/B4 (MkIV) and J/B5-6 (MkV/MkVI) are a US$25.00 upcharge to the standard braces as they are custom fabricated and take up to three weeks to fabricate (but usually faster). It really depends on where we are in a production cycle. The upcharge is because we do not keep these models in stock and the rivet holes and reinforcement tube must to be cut to different lengths to accommodate the Jetta/Bora pass-through to the trunk (boot).

Can I use the UNIbrace XB Series braces as a harness bar?

We are often asked if this is possible. While it may look like this is OK we do not recommend attaching a harness because the brace is not designed as a harness anchoring device and loads the brace and its anchors in ways that it was not designed for. For safety’s sake please use an approved harness bar.

UB Series Questions

I’m not sure which UB model I should purchase?

Please refer to our compatibility guide.

How do I know if I need to purchase the spacer/deep nut kit?

Generally the spacer/deep nut kit is required for aftermarket exhausts that have a 4” Ø, or larger, resonator in the mid pipe area. When in doubt please contact us at info@unibrace.com and we will do our best to assist you with your specific exhaust. Due to the tremendous range of products for 2008+ cars we may not be familiar with all manufacturers, specifically Non-US models, but we will do our best to guide you. When in doubt, especially for International orders, it is always safer to purchase the spacer/deep nut kit so it can be included in your order.

How do I know if I need to purchase the UNIbrace UBxlr instead of the UNIbrace UB?

The UNIbrace UBxlr model was specially designed for the MkVI Golf R with the APR turboback exhaust due to its HUGE resonator. The UNIbrace UBxlr and UNIbrace UB have the same profile and bolt pattern so they will fit the same car models. It may also be useful for custom exhausts with extra large resonators.

How much ground clearance do I loose with the UNIbrace UB/UBQ/UBxlr?

The UNIbrace UB and UNIbrace UBQ reduce clearance approximately 1.125” (28mm). The UNIbrace UBxlr does not reduce ground clearance as the APR RSC turboback hangs lower than the brace.

Will the UNIbrace UB fit TDI models? Do I need the Spacer/Deep Nut Kit? What about the UBQ for the Mk7 models?

For the Golf TDI we have been told by customers that the spacer/deep nut kit is necessary for the emissions equipment so we expect the Jetta is the same. However, for the Audi A3 TDI this does not seem to be the case. When in doubt, especially for International orders, it is always safer to purchase the spacer/deep nut kit so it can be included in your order. For the UNIbrace UBQ there are no additional spacers or hardware required and mounting holes are pre-drilled for the emissions equipment.

I purchased a used UNIbrace UB, but lost or did not have the hardware. Do you sell them?

Yes, please refer to the Tools/Hardware tab at the top of the page. This is to be used only when purchasing tools/hardware without a UNIbrace part order. If you are ordering another UNIbrace part and want to purchase tools/hardware, please contact us at: info@unibrace.com

Do I need to purchase the M8 HD nutsert installation tool for the UB-T?

You do not need to purchase the tool from us, but you absolutely need a proper M8 nutsert installation tool. Please DO NOT make a tool from a standard bolt and nuts. The vast majority of people who have had installation issues have been due to not using a proper tool. These tools, and other nutsert installation tools are made from hardened steel or have a means of getting a large amount of torque needed to set the nutsert properly. Homemade tools do not. We understand not wanting to spend extra money, but you will regret this choice!

Will the UNIbrace UB fit the Volkswagen R36 or Audi MkII TT 3.2?

Unfortunately the UNIbrace UB will not fit with the stock exhausts and their side-by-side catalytic converters even when using the spacer/deep nut kit. It will fit if you are using an aftermarket exhaust with a single catalytic converter less than 4” Ø resonator in the mid pipe area. For larger single catalytic converters please select the spacer/deep nut kit on the tools/hardware page.

Will the UNIbrace UB-T fit the Audi MkII TTRS?

Yes, but not with the secondary catalytic converters. We also recommend selecting the TTRS hardware kit for this model and all 3” Ø or larger exhausts.

What is the UNIbrace UB hardware specification and what is the torque spec?

The Volkswagen OEM nuts are part number: N 907 611 01 M8 shouldered hex nuts. The nuts that are part of the spacer/deep nut kit are custom fabricated to work with the ¼”/7mm spacers and can be purchased on the tools/hardware page. See installation page for torque specification.

RB Series Questions

Will the UNIbrace RB fit all exhausts?

The current version of the UNIbrace RB will fit all exhausts that we are aware of. Our current version is now the third revision of this product. The initial version was designed around the APR and AWE 3” catbacks, however we still developed clearance issues with both stock and some 3” catback exhausts. This was our most challenging product due to its small size and tight location, further complicated by numerous aftermarket exhaust and swaybar combinations. Based on customer feedback we corrected many exhaust problems with version two, and are confident that with version three we have it dialed in perfectly. Persistence paid off! Versions one and two will still fit APR and AWE 3” catback exhausts and all Jetta/Bora installations.
